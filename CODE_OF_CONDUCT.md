# Code of Conduct

## Our Approach

This repository exists to build, maintain, and improve software.

We do not evaluate contributors based on personal identity, beliefs, or affiliations. Topics such as religion, politics, or personal life are outside the scope of this project.

What matters is:

> The quality of your contributions and your ability to collaborate.

In practice:

> Don’t ask. Don’t tell. Just write your code.

This means:

* Personal religious, political, or ideological beliefs are not relevant here
* Personal identity or private matters are not part of project discussions
* No one is expected to disclose such information
* No one should request or introduce such topics into the workspace

We maintain a strictly technical environment where:

> Nobody is judged on who they are — only on what they build.

---

## Expected Behavior

By participating in this repository (issues, pull requests, discussions), you agree to:

* Keep interactions professional and technically focused
* Communicate clearly and concisely
* Provide constructive, actionable feedback
* Base arguments on evidence, not opinion
* Respect maintainers’ decisions

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

To maintain a productive environment, the following are not permitted:

* Personal attacks or insults
* Harassment of any kind
* Political, religious, or ideological debates
* Identity-based arguments or positioning
* Off-topic discussions that derail technical work
* Repeated disregard of project guidelines or maintainer direction

---

## Contribution Guidelines (Behavioral)

When contributing:

* Focus on the problem and the solution
* Critique code, not people
* Keep pull requests scoped and relevant
* Avoid unnecessary discussion overhead
* Accept that not all contributions will be merged

---

## Maintainer Authority

Maintainers are responsible for the direction and integrity of the project.

They may:

* Redirect or close discussions that are off-topic
* Request changes or clarification on contributions
* Reject contributions that do not meet project standards
* Limit or remove participation in case of repeated disruption

Decisions are made based on project needs and are final.

---

## Enforcement

If conduct deviates from this Code:

1. A warning or redirection may be issued
2. Contributions or comments may be removed
3. Access to participation (issues, PRs, discussions) may be restricted

No public escalation process is guaranteed.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

* GitHub Issues
* Pull Requests
* Discussions
* Code Reviews
* Any official communication channels related to this repository

---

## Final Statement

This is a technical workspace.

Not a forum for personal expression.

If you are here, you are here to build.
